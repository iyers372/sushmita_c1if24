He was made the cupbearer to the gods, pouring their nectar not as a symbol of honor, but as a reminder of how even the most beautiful and innocent can be controlled by the ruthless. The gods, indifferent to Ganymede’s suffering, reveled in the power they held over him. To ensure that Ganymede never forgot his place, Zeus turned him into a star, immortalizing him as Aquarius, the Water Bearer—but in this version, it was a cold, impersonal symbol of subjugation

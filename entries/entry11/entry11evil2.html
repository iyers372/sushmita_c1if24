Driven by his own insatiable desires, Zeus transformed into a majestic eagle and swooped down upon Ganymede, abducting him from his home. Far from a gentle ascent to Olympus, it was a violent and unsettling act—a kidnapping, an invasion of Ganymede’s life, an abuse of power masked as favor. Once on Olympus, Ganymede was forced into servitude, not out of respect but out of sheer dominance.
